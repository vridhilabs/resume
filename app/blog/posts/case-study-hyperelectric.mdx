## 1. Client & Context

HyperElectric.in is an EV leasing company operating in the quick commerce and last-mile delivery space. They lease electric vehicles to riders who deliver for major platforms like Zepto, Swiggy, and BigBasket.

The business has two growth vectors:
- **Fleet ops**: Scaling rider onboarding and EV leasing operations
- **Franchise model**: Building a franchisee network for geographic expansion

What they needed was a GTM partner who could handle both simultaneously — without losing operational coherence.

---

## 2. The Workstreams

We structured the engagement across five parallel workstreams:

### 2.1 Rider Onboarding

The rider onboarding process needed to be smooth, compliant, and fast — riders needed to be on the road within days of enquiry, not weeks.

**What we built:**
- Onboarding flow documentation from enquiry → contract → vehicle assignment
- Communication templates for each onboarding stage
- Compliance checklist for EV leasing requirements
- Rider handoff process from sales to ops

**Why it mattered**: Fast onboarding = faster rider activation = faster revenue.

---

### 2.2 EV Vehicles Leasing

The core product needed clear positioning for two audiences:
- Riders looking to join the platform
- Platform partners (Zepto, Swiggy, BigBasket) who needed fleet reliability

**What we built:**
- Vehicle leasing collateral and pitch materials
- Fleet partnership decks for platform negotiations
- Leasing terms documentation for different rider segments
- Pricing structure for multi-vehicle fleet commitments

---

### 2.3 Services Positioning

Beyond the vehicle, HyperElectric offers services that make the leasing stickier. We helped position these as value-adds rather than add-ons.

**What we built:**
- Services packaging framework
- Value proposition for service tiers (maintenance, insurance, breakdown support)
- Competitive differentiation for EV vs. petrol delivery economics

---

### 2.4 Vendor Partnerships (Zepto, Swiggy, BigBasket)

Working with major quick commerce platforms required a B2B pitch that spoke to their operational needs: fleet reliability, rider accountability, and cost efficiency.

**What we built:**
- Platform partnership pitch materials
- Fleet performance SLAs and reporting frameworks
- Rider compliance standards for platform requirements
- Communication cadence for platform key accounts

---

### 2.5 Investor Communications & Website Pipeline

As a growth-stage company, HyperElectric needed investor-facing materials that told a coherent story — plus a website that converted franchise enquiries.

**Investor Comms:**
- Investor presentation deck (narrative, numbers, trajectory)
- Communication templates for investor updates
- Data room structure for due diligence

**Website → Franchise Pipeline:**
- Franchise enquiry form and capture flow
- Documentation hub (what a franchisee needs to know)
- Follow-up sequence for website enquiries
- Lead routing from website → sales team

## 3. The Franchise Enquiry System

One of the most important pieces: connecting the right documentation to get franchise enquiries converted.

**The problem**: Franchise leads were coming in but falling through the cracks because there was no clear "this is what you get" documentation.

**What we built:**
- Franchise enquiry landing page with clear value prop
- Document download hub (investment details, territory maps, FAQ, success metrics)
- Enquiry capture + routing to franchise sales
- Follow-up sequence for stalled enquiries

**Result**: Website became a lead generation asset, not just a digital brochure.

---

## 4. Key Learnings

### EV Fleet GTM is different from SaaS GTM

In SaaS, you're selling software. At HyperElectric, you're selling an operational outcome — "riders on the road, delivering for platforms." The GTM had to speak to operations, not features.

### Franchise GTM requires trust-building materials

Franchisees aren't impulse buyers. They do due diligence. The website needed to answer the question "is this a real, sustainable business?" — not just "sign up here."

### Vendor partnerships need performance data

Zepto, Swiggy, and BigBasket don't care about the EV brand — they care about uptime and cost per delivery. Our materials had to lead with fleet performance data.
