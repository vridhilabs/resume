GTM engineers have a tool problem. Not a shortage — an overdose.

Open any "GTM stack" thread on LinkedIn and you'll see 50+ tools. "You need this CRM, that enrichment tool, these five automation platforms, and oh don't forget your ABM platform."

That's not a stack. That's a migraine.

The reality: most GTM teams can run their entire operation with 12 tools. Not 50. Not 20. Twelve.

Here's the stack that actually works.

## 1. CRM: Twenty CRM (or HubSpot for Enterprise)

You need one source of truth for your pipeline. Not a spreadsheet. Not a "we keep meaning to set it up."

**Twenty CRM** (open-source, free self-hosted):
- Full pipeline management
- Built-in email sequences
- Custom fields and workflows
- API-first for automation

**HubSpot** (if you have budget):
- Better UI for non-technical teams
- Strong marketplace for integrations
- Free tier works for &lt;1,000 contacts

**Skip**: Salesforce (overkill until 100+ reps), Pipedrive (limited automation)

## 2. Enrichment: Clay

Turn an email into a dossier. Clay combines 50+ data sources — Clearbit, Apollo, LinkedIn, Crunchbase, Bombora — into one enrichment engine.

**What it does:**
- Input: email or LinkedIn URL
- Output: job history, tech stack, funding, recent posts, mutual connections

**The play**: Enrich your outbound list before writing copy. The data becomes your personalization layer.

**Skip if**: You have &lt;50 prospects (manual LinkedIn research is fine at that scale)

## 3. Email Automation: Smartlead (or Instantly)

Sending cold email at scale requires infrastructure. Gmail caps you at 500/day. Google Workspace at 2,000/day (but stay under 100 for safety).

**Smartlead**:
- Unlimited inboxes (buy domains for $10/yr each)
- Built-in warm-up (builds domain reputation automatically)
- AI personalization at scale
- Reply detection and auto-stop

**Instantly**: Similar features, slightly cheaper, good UI.

**Skip**: Mailchimp, ConvertKit (these are for opt-in newsletters, not cold outreach)

## 4. Analytics: June.so (or Mixpanel)

You need to know what's working. Not "we got 1,000 visitors" — but "which channel drove pipeline."

**June.so**:
- Connects to Stripe, HubSpot, Google Ads, Meta Ads
- Shows real attribution (not last-touch lies)
- Tracks pipeline, not just conversions
- Clean UI, no SQL required

**Mixpanel**:
- Better for product analytics (user behavior inside your app)
- More complex setup, more powerful queries

**Skip**: Google Analytics 4 (attribution is broken, UI is a maze)

## 5. Content: Notion + MDX

Your GTM motion needs documentation. Playbooks, templates, case studies, and outbound sequences.

**Notion**:
- Team wiki for GTM processes
- Embeddable databases (competitor tracking, campaign calendar)
- Public pages (publish your playbooks as lead magnets)

**MDX/Markdown**:
- Write once, publish everywhere
- Version control with Git
- Perfect for technical GTM teams

**Skip**: Confluence (slow), Google Docs (no structure)

## 6. SEO: Ahrefs (or SEMrush)

If you're doing content-led growth, you need to know what keywords your buyers search for.

**Ahrefs**:
- Keyword explorer (search volume, difficulty, CPC)
- Competitor analysis (what keywords are they ranking for?)
- Backlink analysis (who's linking to you vs. competitors)
- Content gap analysis (keywords you're missing)

**SEMrush**: Similar features, slightly better PPC data.

**Skip if**: You're not investing in SEO (these tools are $200+/month — wait until you have budget)

## 7. Social: Taplio (or Buffer)

LinkedIn is the #1 channel for B2B GTM. But posting manually is a chore.

**Taplio**:
- AI-assisted post generation (give it a topic, get 3 drafts)
- Schedule weeks of content in one sitting
- Analytics on what performed
- Lead generation (find prospects who engaged with your posts)

**Buffer**:
- Cheaper alternative
- Good for multiple social channels (Twitter, LinkedIn, Instagram)

**Skip**: Hootsuite (bloated, expensive), Sprout Social (enterprise-only)

## 8. Forms: Tally (or Typeform)

You need forms for intake, surveys, and lead capture. Google Forms looks amateur.

**Tally**:
- Beautiful forms, free tier is generous
- Logic jumps (show/hide fields based on answers)
- Integrates with everything (Zapier, Make, direct API)
- No branding on free tier

**Typeform**:
- Better animations and UX
- More expensive, but worth it for high-stakes forms (demo requests)

**Skip**: Google Forms (looks cheap), Wufoo (dated UI)

## 9. Scheduling: Cal.com (or Calendly)

You need a way for prospects to book time with you. Don't send "when are you free?" emails back and forth.

**Cal.com**:
- Open-source, self-hostable
- Beautiful UI, custom branding
- Integrates with Google/Outlook calendars
- Free for basic use

**Calendly**:
- More polished UI
- Better team scheduling features
- Free tier works for individuals

**Skip**: YouCanBook.me (dated), HubSpot Meetings (requires HubSpot subscription)

## 10. Payments: Stripe (or Razorpay for India)

If you're selling, you need to get paid. Don't send invoices via PayPal.

**Stripe**:
- Industry standard for SaaS/subscriptions
- API-first (integrate with your CRM, automate billing)
- Global coverage, supports 135+ currencies
- Stripe Checkout (embeddable payment form in 5 minutes)

**Razorpay**:
- Better for Indian market
- UPI, net banking, wallets support
- Similar API quality to Stripe

**Skip**: PayPal (terrible UX, higher fees), Wire transfers (slow, manual)

## 11. Automation: Zapier (or Make)

You need to connect tools without writing code. When someone fills your form → create CRM lead → send Slack notification → add to email sequence.

**Zapier**:
- 5,000+ app integrations
- No-code interface (drag and drop)
- Reliability (99.9% uptime)
- Free tier: 100 tasks/month

**Make** (formerly Integromat):
- More powerful logic (branching, iterators, data stores)
- Cheaper than Zapier for high volume
- Steeper learning curve

**Skip**: n8n (requires self-hosting unless you pay), Tray.io (enterprise-only)

## 12. Data: Google Sheets (or Airtable)

The glue that holds your stack together. Every GTM team needs a spreadsheet for something.

**Google Sheets**:
- Free, collaborative, API access
- Pivot tables, formulas, charts
- Connects to Zapier/Make for automation
- 10M cells per sheet (you won't hit this limit)

**Airtable**:
- Spreadsheet + database hybrid
- Better for structured data (CRM-lite, project tracking)
- Expensive for large datasets

**Skip**: Excel (not cloud-native), Notion databases (slow for large datasets)

## The "Nice to Have" List (Add Later)

Once you're at $50K+ ARR and have budget:

1. **Apollo** — Better data than Clay for high-volume prospecting
2. **ZoomInfo** — Enterprise contact database ($$$ but comprehensive)
3. **Salesloft** — Sales engagement platform (cadence automation)
4. **Gong** — Conversation intelligence (records and analyzes calls)
5. **Demandbase** — Account-based marketing (target companies, not just people)

Don't buy these early. They're expensive and require mature processes to use effectively.

## Stack Cost Calculator

| Stage | Tools | Monthly Cost |
|-------|-------|---------------|
| **Lean** (0-$10K ARR) | Twenty CRM, Tally, Cal.com, Google Sheets | $0 |
| **Growing** ($10K-$50K ARR) | Add: Clay, Smartlead, June.so, Zapier | ~$300 |
| **Scale** ($50K+ ARR) | Upgrade: HubSpot, Ahrefs, Taplio, Make | ~$800 |

The point: you can run a serious GTM motion for under $300/month. Don't let vendors convince you otherwise.

## Try This: Audit Your Current Stack

Spend 20 minutes this week:

1. **List every tool** you're paying for (including that $29/mo tool you forgot about)
2. **Mark each as**: essential / nice-to-have / unused
3. **Cancel the "unused" column** — save that cash for customer acquisition
4. **Check for overlaps** — are you paying for two tools that do the same thing?

Most teams find $200-500/month in wasted subscriptions. That's a conference sponsorship or 10,000 cold emails right there.

## Summary

A GTM stack doesn't need to be impressive — it needs to work. Start with the 12 essentials, add "nice-to-haves" only when you have budget and process maturity. Most teams waste $1,000+/month on tools they don't use. Audit your stack quarterly and kill what's not driving pipeline.
