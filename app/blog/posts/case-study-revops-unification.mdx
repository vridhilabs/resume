## 1. Client & Context

The company is a global SaaS platform serving 50,000+ customers across 150+ countries. They provide CRM, customer support, and marketing automation tools — all in one suite.

At $5-10M ARR (Series B), they were struggling with:
- **Three disconnected data worlds**: Sales in HubSpot, Support in their own product, Marketing in... everywhere.
- **No single source of truth**: Leadership couldn't answer "what's our real CAC?" in under 3 days.
- **Leaking pipeline**: Leads stuck between marketing automation and sales CRM with no routing logic.

What they needed wasn't "more leads" — it was **RevOps foundations** that could unify data across products.

---

## 2. The Problem

When we audited their RevOps, four things were broken:

- **CRM hygiene disaster**: HubSpot had 12,000+ leads with 40% duplicates, 30% missing firmographics, and no lead scoring.
- **No data unification**: Sales didn't know if a lead was also a support ticket (or vice versa). Cross-sell opportunities were invisible.
- **Broken handoffs**: MQL → SQL conversion was 12% (industry avg: 20-25%). Reason: "marketing qualified" meant "filled a form" — not "ready for sales."
- **Zero dashboards**: Leadership was making decisions off 3 different spreadsheets that contradicted each other.

Baseline metrics:
- MQL → SQL: 12%
- CAC payback: 14 months (benchmark: 8-12 months for Series B)
- NRR: 92% (benchmark: 100-110%)
- Pipeline visibility: 60% (40% of deals "invisible" in spreadsheets)

---

## 3. Our Diagnosis

We ran a 14-day diagnostic across three lenses:

**Funnel view**: Leads were dying at the "marketing → sales" handoff. The 12% MQL→SQL wasn't a sales problem — it was a definition problem.

**Data view**: No connection between:
- Support ticket volume → churn risk
- Marketing email engagement → sales readiness
- Product usage → expansion opportunity

**RevOps view**: HubSpot was underutilized (using 20% of features), no automated routing, no lead scoring, no attribution model.

The diagnosis was clear: **they had grown past their RevOps infrastructure**. Time to rebuild.

---

## 4. The Strategy

Our 90-day RevOps sprint had four pillars:

**Pillar 1: CRM Foundation**
- Clean HubSpot (remove duplicates, enrich missing data)
- Implement lead scoring (demographic + behavioral)
- Build routing logic (inbound, outbound, partner routes)

**Pillar 2: Data Unification**
- Connect product usage data → CRM (via API)
- Connect support ticket data → CRM (Zendesk integration)
- Build "customer 360" view in HubSpot

**Pillar 3: Funnel Re-engineering**
- Redefine MQL (add behavioral criteria: 3+ product logins, 2+ pricing page visits)
- Implement lead lifecycle stages (MQL → SAL → SQL → Opportunity)
- Build automated nurturing for "not ready" leads

**Pillar 4: Dashboards & Reporting**
- CEO dashboard: ARR, NRR, pipeline, CAC payback
- RevOps dashboard: MQL→SQL, CAC by channel, lead response time
- Sales dashboard: My pipeline, next actions, at-risk deals

---

## 5. Execution

### 5.1 CRM Cleanup

**Week 1-2**: 
- Exported 12,000+ leads from HubSpot
- Used **Clay** to identify duplicates (email + company matching)
- Merged 3,400 duplicates, archived 2,800 "dead" leads (no activity in 12+ months)
- Enriched 6,000+ leads with Clearbit (firmographics, tech stack)

**Week 3-4**:
- Implemented **lead scoring** (0-100 points):
  - Demographic: Company size (20 pts), Industry match (15 pts), Funding (15 pts)
  - Behavioral: Product usage (25 pts), Pricing page visit (10 pts), Content download (5 pts)
  - Threshold for MQL: 50+ points

Result: 6,000 high-quality leads (from 12,000). Sales stopped wasting time on 0-point leads.

### 5.2 Data Unification

**Week 5-6**: 
- Built API integration: Product usage data → HubSpot (daily sync)
- Integrated Zendesk: Support ticket count + satisfaction score → HubSpot
- Created "Customer 360" custom objects in HubSpot:
  - Total MRR
  - Product tier
  - Support ticket count (last 90 days)
  - Product login frequency

**Result**: Sales could now see: "This lead has 3 support tickets open and hasn't logged in 14 days — at-risk for churn."

### 5.3 Funnel Re-engineering

**Week 7-8**:
- Redefined MQL criteria (added behavioral):
  - Old: "Filled a form" → MQL
  - New: "50+ lead score OR 3+ product logins OR requested demo" → MQL

- Built automated nurturing sequences (for leads <50 points):
  - Week 1: Educational content (case studies, ROI calculator)
  - Week 2-4: Product education (how-to guides, webinar invites)
  - Week 5+: Sales outreach (only if score reaches 50+)

**Result**: MQL → SQL jumped from 12% → 28%.

### 5.4 Dashboards & Reporting

**Week 9-12**:
- **CEO dashboard** (weekly automated report):
  - ARR, NRR, GRR
  - Pipeline created (this week vs. last week)
  - CAC payback by channel
  - Forecast: This quarter vs. next quarter

- **RevOps dashboard** (daily):
  - MQL → SQL conversion (rolling 30 days)
  - Lead response time (average, by rep)
  - Pipeline velocity (days in each stage)
  - CAC by channel (with attribution model)

- **Sales dashboard** (real-time):
  - My pipeline (by stage)
  - Next best action (AI-suggested)
  - At-risk deals (no activity in 7+ days)

## 6. Results

| Metric | Before | After | Benchmark (Series B SaaS) |
|--------|--------|-------|----------------------------|
| MQL → SQL | 12% | 28% | 20-25% |
| CAC payback | 14 months | 11 months | 8-12 months |
| NRR | 92% | 108% | 100-110% |
| Pipeline visibility | 60% | 82% | 75-80% |
| Lead response time | 48 hours | 4 hours | 2-4 hours |
| CRM data quality | 60% complete | 95% complete | 85-90% |

**What improved most**: The MQL→SQL jump (12% → 28%) created a cascade effect — sales had better leads, closed faster, and CAC dropped.

**Benchmark context**: At Series B, typical NRR is 100-110%. We ended at 108%, which is in the "strong" band. CAC payback of 11 months is slightly above average (benchmark: 8-12 months), but we improved it by 3 months in 90 days.

**Pipeline visibility** of 82% means leadership could finally see where deals were stuck. The 40% "invisible pipeline" was now tracked in HubSpot.

## 8. Takeaways for Other Founders

- **Don't scale RevOps too early**: This company tried to build "Salesforce-level" RevOps at $2M ARR. Start with HubSpot + clean data first.
- **MQL definition is everything**: If you're at 12% MQL→SQL, redefine "qualified" before buying more leads.
- **Data unification compounds**: Connecting product + support + CRM data unlocked 22% more pipeline visibility in 90 days.
- **Dashboards drive behavior**: When sales reps saw "lead response time: 48 hours" on their dashboard, they fixed it to 4 hours.
